# Plan

The goal is to keep two people going long enough to actually speak Croatian
(Dalmatian) together — by making the app survive daily life and making it theirs,
not by building a bigger course.

Order matters: **keep it alive first, make it yours second, grow content last.**

## 1. Keep it alive

The real risk isn't missing content. It's quitting in week two. So reduce
friction and stop the streak from punishing.

- **Forgiving streak.** A missed day shouldn't kill it. Add a grace day (or a
  couple of "freezes" a week). And don't make one person's habit hostage to the
  other: count a day when each of you has practised within a short window, not
  only when you both go on the same calendar day.
- **Co-op over competition.** The weekly duel pits you against each other, which
  falls flat the moment you're not matched. Make it optional, or replace it with
  a shared weekly goal you hit together.
- **Optional lock.** The household passphrase is one more secret to type on every
  device. Default it off; offer it to anyone who wants it. One fewer setup step.
- **Simpler second-device setup.** The gist connect should be one clear screen.
  Show the gist id plainly (and as a QR) so the other phone joins by scanning
  instead of typing.

## 2. Make it yours

This is the part no other app gives you, and the reason to use this one over
Duolingo.

- **"Our phrases" deck.** Add a Croatian phrase and its meaning in a few taps; it
  goes into the same review engine as everything else. Your words, not just the
  course's.
- **Record real voices.** A record button on any card — your own voice, or your
  nona's — played instead of the synthetic one. This is also the fix for phones
  with no Croatian voice installed, and for Dalmatian a recording is closer to
  right than the standard voice anyway. Start with recordings kept on the device;
  sharing them between phones can come later.
- **Keep the family-corrections loop** (flag → fix → relearn). It already works.
  Small touch: show who fixed what, so it feels shared.

## 3. Do it together, and use it in life

The one thing a couple can do that a lone learner can't.

- **Quiz-each-other mode.** One phone shows a prompt, the other says the answer
  out loud, you tap right or wrong. The one genuinely-together activity.
- **A small daily nudge:** "try saying this to each other today," drawn from words
  you already know.

## 4. Grow the content — slowly, and last

- The "answer back" exercise (hear a question, give the reply) is the next real
  teaching step, when you want it.
- Otherwise let the language grow through the phrases you actually add and the
  corrections from family, rather than a syllabus.

## What depends on you

If one of you is well ahead, the stronger one becomes the one who records audio,
adds phrases and corrects — and the co-op streak matters more than any duel. If
you're roughly level, the shared daily habit is the spine. Either way, Phase 1
holds.

## Where to start

Phase 1 — the streak and setup changes. They're small, they're the difference
between this surviving past a fortnight, and they don't depend on the unknowns.
